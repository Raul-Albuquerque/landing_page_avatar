About

Avatar: The Way of Water" launches the story of the Sully family (Jake, Neytiri, and their kids), the trouble that follows them, the lengths they go to keep each other safe, the battles they fight to stay alive, and the tragedies they endure.

All of this against the breathtaking backdrop of Pandora, where audiences are introduced to new Na’vi cultures and a range of exotic sea creatures that populate the majestic oceans. Nominated for numerous Academy Awards® including Best Picture, the James Cameron-directed film became the third highest-grossing box office film of all-time and set a new benchmark for visual effects.

Directed by: James Cameron.

Curiosities

  • One actor declined to be in the movie
    • Edward Norton said that he was offered a role in “The Way of Water,” but he turned it down. He said it was because he only wanted to play a Na’vi and was offered a human role. Given Norton and Cameron’s respective reputations for being controlling and tempestuous, this was perhaps for the best.
  • Kate Winslet broke a record
    • Winslet and Cameron reunited after working together, iconically, on “Titanic.” It wasn’t always the most straightforward shoot back then, and Winslet had hesitation about jumping back in. When she did, she found herself being asked to submerge herself and hold her breath time and time again.
    • However, she’s actually quite good at that, apparently. Winslet held her breath underwater for seven minutes, a new record for film production. A big caveat, sure, but impressive anyway.
  • It came out of the gate strong
    • With a budget of somewhere between $350 and $460 million based on reporting, “The Way of Water” is either one of the most expensive movies ever made or the most expensive. Cameron made no bones that his film would have to bring in a ton of cash even to break even.
    • It started quite strong, but given the circumstances, maybe not as strong as hoped. “Avatar 2” made $441.7 million worldwide in its opening weekend. That’s great, of course, and in fact the 11th-highest opening of all time. It also was the third-highest-grossing opening weekend since the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • We are getting a sequel
    • Part of the delay in getting “The Way of Water” into theaters is that Cameron filmed “Avatar 3” simultaneously. Yes, like a regular Peter Jackson, Cameron did double duty while filming. There are rumors the third film will have the subtitle “The Seed Bearer,” and it is scheduled to be released on December 20, 2024. So yeah, less of a wait this time.
